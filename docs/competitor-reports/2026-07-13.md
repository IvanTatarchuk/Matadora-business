# Competitor Research: ConTech / Construction-Management SaaS in Poland & CEE

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Author:** Automated research (Claude Code)
**Purpose:** Follow-up survey of the competitive landscape for construction-management SaaS in Poland and neighboring EU/CEE markets, benchmarked against Matadora's core modules (VAT-aware offer/estimate builder, investor↔contractor↔wholesaler marketplace, KSeF e-invoicing, multi-role dashboards). This is a follow-up to the [2026-07-06 report](./2026-07-06.md) — it revisits the international players, confirms/updates prior findings, and adds two Polish direct competitors not previously identified.

Research was performed via web search; where a source could not be directly verified (site blocked direct fetch, or only a single secondary source existed), it is flagged inline as unverified/uncertain rather than stated as fact.

## 1. New since last week: closer Polish direct competitors

The most significant finding this cycle is two Poland-native, all-in-one platforms that were missed in the prior report and sit noticeably closer to Matadora's actual product shape than anything previously surveyed:

**budow.app** — a mobile-first, browser-based (no app-store install) system for contractors, general contractors, developers, and independent estimators. Combines construction diary (dziennik budowy), estimating (kosztorysowanie), scheduling, inventory/warehouse (magazyn), and **native KSeF integration** in one product. The KSeF integration authenticates via a company token and auto-pulls incoming cost invoices into an "unassigned invoices" queue; each invoice is then tagged to a project/stage/task and the budget updates immediately — a tighter invoice-to-budget loop than Matadora currently appears to expose. Direct page fetch was blocked (403), so detail here is search-snippet-derived (moderately verified) — pricing was not found. No evidence of a multi-contractor bidding marketplace or a wholesaler role.
Source: [budow.app](https://budow.app/)

**BudoApka** — targets small contractors and finishing/renovation crews plus their investor-clients. Digital construction journal, task-based coordination, budget/quote tools, and a distinctive investor-facing feature: clients get a unique view-only link (no account/password needed) to see project reports on the contractor's schedule. Field UX is optimized for one-handed/gloved use (adding a photo+note in ~10-15s). Pricing is flat and transparent: **139 PLN/month per company** (marketed as "6 PLN/day"), notably simpler and cheaper than Buildertrend/Procore-style per-tier or volume pricing. No bidding marketplace, no wholesaler role, no KSeF mentioned.
Sources: [BudoApka](https://budoapka.pl/), [PLNDesign.pl coverage](https://plndesign.pl/architektura/koniec-z-chaosem-na-budowie-polacy-stworzyli-budoapke-ktora-zmieni-branze/)

**Why this matters:** budow.app in particular narrows the gap the prior report flagged as Matadora's moat — it already pairs VAT-relevant estimating with live KSeF invoice ingestion in a single Polish product, which is closer to Matadora's estimate→finance loop than Comarch/InsERT (back-office ERPs) or PlanRadar/Fieldwire (field tools only). Neither budow.app nor BudoApka has a multi-sided bidding marketplace or a wholesaler role, so Matadora's three-role marketplace claim still holds — but the "nobody combines estimating + KSeF" half of that claim is now weaker than last week's report stated.

## 2. Re-confirmed findings from prior report (condensed)

- **PlanRadar** (Austria, Warsaw office since 2020): field documentation/defect-tracking for GCs, subs, FM, architects; ~$32–$159/user/mo; no VAT estimating, no KSeF, no marketplace.
- **Procore**: enterprise/volume-based pricing (~0.1-0.2% of annual construction volume, often $4,500-$25,000+/yr); no confirmed Polish office or localization; commentary suggests it's oversized/costly for Polish SMB contractors.
- **Buildertrend** ($339-$829/mo tiered as of mid-2026) and **Fieldwire by Hilti** ($39-$104/user/mo tiered, free tier up to 5 users): both remain US/English-market-focused mid-market field/PM tools with no confirmed PLN pricing or Polish localization.
- **Autodesk Construction Cloud / Autodesk Build**: enterprise BIM-centric, custom/tiered pricing not published in Poland; weak single-source evidence of a local reseller channel; commentary notes ACC licensing is viewed as expensive for Polish market conditions. No VAT/KSeF/marketplace features.
- **Norma PRO/EXPERT (Athenasoft)** and **Datacomp Zuzia/BIMestiMate**: entrenched single-user desktop estimating tools with deep KNR-catalog norm databases; no marketplace, no native KSeF, no multi-role dashboards — but the incumbency/switching-cost problem for professional kosztorysanci remains real.
- **Wayman**, **Comarch ERP**, **InsERT/Symfonia**: Polish ERP/back-office incumbents. Comarch's free/subsidized KSeF rollout through 2026 continues to compress KSeF from a differentiator into table stakes.
- **Fixly (OLX Group)**, public-tender aggregators (Eurobudowa, Platforma Zakupowa, Atlas Przetargów, **StartInvest**, **Kompas Inwestycji** — newly noted this cycle), and single-sided B2B wholesale ordering platforms (3W, KIM24, Automico): marketplace/lead-gen or procurement-only, not full estimate→bid→execute→invoice loops.

Full detail on each of the above is unchanged from the [2026-07-06 report](./2026-07-06.md); see that file for sourcing.

## 3. Trend update

**KSeF mandate is now live for large taxpayers.** As of this writing, the Feb 1, 2026 deadline for ~4,200 large taxpayers has passed and the broader April 1, 2026 mandate covering most VAT-registered businesses is imminent/in effect, with the Jan 1, 2027 deadline still ahead for micro-enterprises. This raises the urgency (relative to last week) of Matadora's KSeF module being not just present but reliable in production — every competitor surveyed that touches invoicing (Comarch, InsERT, budow.app) is racing the same clock.
Sources: [Dudkowiak & Putyra — E-Invoicing in Poland](https://www.dudkowiak.com/tax-law-in-poland/e-invoicing-in-poland-ksef/), [Fiskaly — KSeF 2026 mandate](https://www.fiskaly.com/blog/ksef-e-invoicing-poland-pos-erp-providers/)

**ConTech funding is up but not flowing to Poland.** Q1 2026 global ConTech investment hit ~$1.85B across 68 deals (AI-focused ConTech alone at $521M, the highest since 2021), but European activity is concentrated in Austria, the UK, and Germany — no Poland-specific ConTech funding was found. This suggests Polish incumbents (SimplyBud, budow.app, BudoApka, Connecto) are largely bootstrapped/self-funded rather than VC-backed, which may mean slower feature velocity than a funded entrant could bring — a window for Matadora if it can move faster.
Source: [Cemex Ventures — Q1 2026 ConTech Investment Trends](https://www.cemexventures.com/contech-investment-trends-q1-2026/)

## 4. Suggestions (updated)

1. **Re-verify the KSeF invoice-to-budget loop against budow.app's bar, not just against ERP incumbents.** budow.app's "invoice auto-pulled from KSeF → assign to project/stage/task → budget updates instantly" flow is a concrete, checkable UX bar. Confirm Matadora's own KSeF module (`src/lib/ksef/`) closes this loop with equally few steps from invoice receipt to project cost attribution — this is now a same-market, same-language competitor to benchmark against directly, not just a hypothetical.

2. **Consider a simple, transparent flat-fee tier alongside any volume/seat-based pricing.** BudoApka's 139 PLN/month flat "per company" pricing is a legible, low-friction offer for the smallest Polish contractors/investors that a tiered-by-feature or per-seat model (Buildertrend/Procore-style) doesn't match. A comparably simple entry tier could lower adoption friction for small contractors evaluating Matadora against BudoApka/SimplyBud specifically.

3. **Lead marketing/positioning with the marketplace + wholesaler combination, since that gap is now the clearest surviving differentiator.** With budow.app narrowing the estimating+KSeF gap, the three-sided investor↔contractor↔wholesaler bidding marketplace (still absent from every competitor found, Polish or international) is the most defensible unique claim left standing — worth being explicit about in outward-facing materials rather than leading with KSeF or estimating alone.
