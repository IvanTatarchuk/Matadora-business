## 1. New this cycle: the KSeF grace period is closing, and FA(3) has a sharp failure mode

The most consequential update since last week is regulatory, not competitive, but it directly affects every product surveyed (Matadora included) that touches Polish invoicing:

- **The penalty grace period for KSeF non-compliance ends 31 July 2026.** Large taxpayers (>PLN 200M revenue) have been mandated since 1 February 2026, and the broader mandate covering essentially all VAT-registered businesses took effect 1 April 2026 — but enforcement of penalties has been held off until end of July. From 20 July 2026 (today), that's roughly **11 days** before penalties become live. Offline/fallback invoicing (issue outside KSeF, submit within a day) remains permitted through 31 December 2026, so it's not a hard cliff, but it's the last low-stakes window for any product's KSeF module to be exercised in anger before real penalty exposure begins.
  Source: [The Invoicing Hub — KSeF 2.0 and FA(3) finalized](https://www.theinvoicinghub.com/ksef-2-0-and-fa3-finalized-time-for-businesses-to-start-implementation/)

- **FA(3) has a documented silent-rejection failure mode.** The new mandatory schema (replacing FA(2) as of this year) validates NIP checksums, requires ISO 8601 dates, restricts VAT rate codes to a fixed Ministry-of-Finance dictionary, and rounds amounts via a specific KSeF algorithm — a one-grosz rounding mismatch is enough to cause rejection. Multiple integration guides note that if a software provider hasn't updated to FA(3), invoices are rejected **without a meaningful end-user warning**. This is a concrete, checkable bar for `src/lib/ksef/`: it's not enough that Matadora's KSeF module submits invoices — it needs to be verified against FA(3)'s specific validation rules (NIP check-digit, date format, VAT code dictionary, rounding algorithm) and needs to surface rejections clearly rather than failing silently.
  Sources: [ksefgpt.pl — XML and the FA(3) schema](https://ksefgpt.pl/en/news/guide/xml-and-the-fa-3-schema-in-ksef-a-guide-for-businesses), [RTC Suite — Poland Introduces FA(3)](https://rtcsuite.com/poland-introduces-fa3-whats-changing-in-the-new-ksef-e-invoicing-schema/)

- **No new direct Polish competitor surfaced this cycle.** A repeat check on budow.app and BudoApka (the two Poland-native all-in-one platforms flagged last week) found no material product or pricing changes since 2026-07-13, and no new Poland-specific ConTech entrant or funding round turned up in this pass — Polish incumbents still appear to be bootstrapped rather than VC-backed, consistent with the prior report.

## 2. Re-confirmed findings from prior reports (condensed)

- **budow.app** — Polish, browser-based, all-in-one for contractors/GCs/developers/estimators: construction diary, kosztorysowanie, scheduling, warehouse, and native KSeF integration with an "unassigned invoices" queue that ties incoming cost invoices to project/stage/task and updates budget immediately. No confirmed bidding marketplace or wholesaler role. Pricing not published; direct page fetch still blocked (403) as of this pass.
- **BudoApka** — Polish, small-contractor/renovation focus, flat **139 PLN/month per company** pricing, investor-facing view-only report links (no login), glove-friendly field UX. No KSeF, no marketplace, no wholesaler role.
- **PlanRadar** (Austria, Warsaw office): field documentation/defect-tracking, ~$35–$179/user/mo (Basic/Starter/Pro tiers; confirmed this pass), Enterprise custom-quoted. No VAT estimating, no KSeF, no marketplace.
- **Procore**: pricing tied to Annual Construction Volume + selected product modules, sales-quote only, unlimited users. No confirmed Polish office/localization.
- **Buildertrend**: ~$299/mo+ (unlimited users, tiered, sales-call required for full quote). US/English-market focused.
- **Fieldwire by Hilti**: Free tier (≤5 users/3 projects), Pro $39/user/mo, Business $64/user/mo, Business Plus $89/user/mo (all annual billing, confirmed this pass). US/English-market focused, no PLN pricing.
- **Autodesk Construction Cloud / Build**: enterprise BIM-centric, custom pricing, weak evidence of Polish reseller presence; viewed as expensive for the Polish market.
- **Comarch ERP / Comarch e-Invoicing**, **InsERT/Symfonia**: Polish back-office ERP incumbents with KSeF connectivity baked in; Comarch continues to compress KSeF integration from differentiator to table stakes across the market.
- **Norma PRO/EXPERT (Athenasoft)**, **Datacomp Zuzia/BIMestiMate**: entrenched single-user desktop KNR-catalog estimating tools; no marketplace, no native KSeF, no multi-role dashboards, but real switching-cost incumbency among professional kosztorysanci.
- **SimplyBud, BrickControl, Petrosoft, Connecto/System Neon, KOFIO, SCCOT** (newly catalogued Polish general construction-management/estimating tools, first surfaced this pass but functionally consistent with the ERP/estimating-tool category above): browser-based estimating and project/cost tracking for Polish construction firms. None found to combine a multi-sided bidding marketplace with a wholesaler role; feature depth on VAT/KSeF varies by vendor and wasn't independently verified for each.
- **Marketplace/tender aggregators**: Eurobudowa.pl, Platforma Zakupowa, Oferent.com.pl, OnePlace/Marketplanet, plus previously noted Fixly (OLX Group), Atlas Przetargów, StartInvest, Kompas Inwestycji. These are lead-gen/tender-listing or public-procurement platforms, not integrated estimate→bid→execute→invoice products.

## 3. Trend update

**KSeF is now a compliance deadline, not a roadmap item — for Matadora and every competitor alike.** With penalty enforcement starting 31 July 2026, the market pressure has shifted from "does your product support KSeF" (most do, at least nominally) to "does your KSeF integration survive FA(3) validation without silently dropping invoices." This is a narrow but real window where correctness, not feature breadth, is the differentiator.

**Global ConTech funding remains healthy but Poland-blind.** Six ConTech startups (Fyld, XBuild, Brickanta, and others) raised a combined $126M in early 2026, and AI-focused ConTech funding hit $521M in Q1 2026 — the highest since 2021. No Poland-specific ConTech funding was identified in this pass, reinforcing the prior report's read that local players (SimplyBud, budow.app, BudoApka, Connecto) are self-funded and likely slower-moving than a resourced entrant.
Sources: [Construction Dive — 6 contech firms raise $126M](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/contech-funding-fyld-sensera-xbuild-moab-payra/814452/)

## 4. Suggestions (updated)

1. **Treat the 31 July 2026 KSeF penalty deadline as a hard verification checkpoint for `src/lib/ksef/`, not a soft target.** Specifically confirm: (a) NIP check-digit validation, (b) ISO 8601 date formatting, (c) VAT rate codes matching the current Ministry dictionary, (d) rounding logic matching KSeF's algorithm, and (e) that a rejected invoice surfaces a clear, actionable error to the user rather than failing silently — this last point is the specific failure mode flagged across multiple integration guides this cycle.

2. **Keep the invoice-to-budget loop benchmark against budow.app live, and re-test it after any KSeF module changes.** No change to that competitor's product was found this cycle, so last week's suggestion still stands unaltered: verify that a KSeF-ingested invoice in Matadora reaches project/stage cost attribution in as few steps as budow.app's "unassigned invoices" queue.

3. **Continue to lead positioning with the marketplace + wholesaler combination.** With no new direct competitor found this cycle and KSeF trending toward commodity status industry-wide, the three-sided investor↔contractor↔wholesaler bidding marketplace remains the most defensible differentiator identified across three consecutive research passes — it's worth being explicit about this in outward-facing materials rather than leading with KSeF or estimating alone, both of which are converging toward table stakes.
